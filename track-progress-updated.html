Understanding Your Progress Metrics

The Ankle Foot Exerciser® rehabilitation process can be tracked using several key metrics:

1. Exercise Mastery

An exercise is considered "mastered" when you can maintain both squares in the horizontal level (sides not touching the floor) for at least 15 seconds, and you can perform the exercise 3 times in a row on each foot.

2. Range of Motion

Tracking your ankle's range of motion in all directions (dorsiflexion, plantarflexion, inversion, and eversion) will show you meaningful improvements in mobility.

3. Balance Duration

The length of time you can maintain balance on the Ankle Foot Exerciser® is a direct indicator of improved proprioception and stability.

4. Pain Levels

Recording your pain levels (0-10 scale) before and after exercises helps track your healing progress and identify which movements need more attention.

5. Functional Milestones

Note when you reach important functional goals like walking without pain, climbing stairs comfortably, or returning to sports activities.

6. Advanced Performance

For athletes and active individuals, track specific performance metrics relevant to your sport or activities.

Rehabilitation Timeline

Every rehabilitation journey is unique, but this general timeline will help you set realistic expectations and track your progress:

Weeks 1-2

Foundation Phase

Focus: Improving basic balance, reducing pain and swelling

Goals: Master exercises 1-4, achieve 10-second balance

Expected Progress: Reduction in pain, improved confidence in movements

Weeks 3-4

Stability Building Phase

Focus: Developing hindfoot and forefoot stability

Goals: Master exercises 5-12, achieve 15-second balance

Expected Progress: Reduction in instability, improved walking comfort

Weeks 5-6

Strengthening Phase

Focus: Building muscle strength, improving coordination

Goals: Master exercises 13-19, begin Excellence Circuit

Expected Progress: Better coordination, ready for more challenging activities

Weeks 7-8

Advanced Skills Phase

Focus: Developing lateral stability, sport-specific movements

Goals: Master exercises 20-25, perform Excellence Circuit S1-S4

Expected Progress: Return to most normal activities, improved athletic performance

Week 9+

Maintenance & Performance

Focus: Maintaining gains, preventing recurrence

Goals: Regular practice of all exercises, continued improvement

Expected Progress: Return to full activity with improved stability and performance

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I track my progress?

Track your progress daily during exercise sessions. Record pain levels, exercise duration, and any improvements you notice. Weekly measurements of range of motion and monthly photos can help visualize long-term progress.

What if my progress seems slow?

Recovery timelines vary greatly between individuals. Focus on consistency rather than speed. Small improvements in balance time, reduced pain, or increased range of motion are all positive signs. Consult your healthcare provider if you have concerns.

Should I continue tracking after recovery?

Yes! Maintenance tracking helps prevent re-injury and maintains the gains you've achieved. Many users continue with 3x weekly sessions and monthly progress checks.

Can I share my progress with my healthcare provider?

Absolutely! The tracking sheets are designed to be shared with physical therapists, doctors, and other healthcare providers. They provide valuable objective data about your recovery progress.
