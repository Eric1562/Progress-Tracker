Fill in the tables below to monitor your progress with the Ankle Foot Exerciser®. Record the date, exercises performed, and your performance metrics. Consistent tracking will help you identify improvements and stay motivated throughout your rehabilitation journey.

Mastery criteria: An exercise is considered "mastered" when you can maintain both squares in the horizontal level (sides not touching the floor) for at least 15 seconds, 3 times in a row on each foot.

Always exercise both feet equally: Even if only one ankle is injured, training both sides is essential. This balanced approach provides a direct comparison between your injured and healthy ankle, offering valuable context for recovery progress. It also helps prevent compensatory movement patterns and muscle imbalances that could lead to future issues. Your healthy ankle serves as your personal benchmark for what "normal" looks and feels like for your body.

Range of Motion Tracking

How to Measure Range of Motion:

Dorsiflexion: Sit with leg extended. Pull foot up toward shin. Normal range: 20°

Plantarflexion: Sit with leg extended. Point toes away from shin. Normal range: 50°

Inversion: Sit with leg extended. Turn sole of foot inward. Normal range: 35°

Eversion: Sit with leg extended. Turn sole of foot outward. Normal range: 25°

Tips: Use a smartphone goniometer app for accurate measurements, or estimate visually comparing to normal range.

Knee to Wall/Heel Distance Test

How to Perform the Test:

  1. Place your foot perpendicular to a wall (toes pointing at wall)
  2. Keep heel firmly on ground throughout the test
  3. Bend knee forward until it touches the wall
  4. Gradually move foot backward until you can just touch the wall with your knee while keeping heel down
  5. Measure distance from wall to big toe (cm)
Normal range: 10-12 cm for healthy adults. Less than 5 cm indicates significant restriction.
